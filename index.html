The Challenge


How do we work with very large raster data?

The Data

National Land Cover Dataset

National Land Cover Dataset

  • Covers entire conterminous US at 30 meter resolution
  • Derived from Landsat data (satellite remote sensing data archived by the USGS).
  • Classifies land into 20 categories, such as "Developed High Intensity" or "Evergreen Forest"

National Land Cover Dataset

  • 161,190 columns by 104,424 rows
  • Uncompressed is approximately 134 GB

Global Circulation Models

Model output data for predicting world temperature and precipitation out to 2099.

Global Circulation Models

  • The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) publishes an assessment reports that reviews and endorses the currently available climate data.
  • The most recent report, AR5, was published in 2014.
  • More than 800 authors were involved in writing the report.
  • This data is important. It takes the abstract concept of "climate change" and describes it with specific measurements. Those measurements are predictions, and are the best guesses by the smartest minds the world has working on the problem.

Global Circulation Models

  • 21 Models
  • 4 Carbon emission scenarios
  • 4 Measurements (Temperature Max, Mean, Min, and Precipitation)
  • Daily model output from 2005 to 2099 (34,310 days)
  • 11,528,160 combinations.

    • The size of each layer depends on the resolution. For a 1000x1000 raster covering the entire world, which is roughly 4 MB, that means the entire dataset would be around 46 Terabytes

The Tools

  • fast and general engine for large-scale data processing
  • does things Hadoop doesn't, like cache intermediate results in memory.
  • written in Scala!
  • Scala library for doing all things geospatial.
  • framework for doing distributed raster processing on Akka and Spark.
  • Currently in incubation at

Accumulo

  • Big table implementation
  • Has sorted indexing.
  • Columnar database.
  • Also used by GeoMesa, another Scala project at LocationTech.

What can we do with Geotrellis and Spark?

  • Work with spatial-only or time-series rasters.

  • Ingest raster data with an indexed tile scheme into Accumulo or HDFS.

  • Server tiles out of HDFS or Accumulo fast enough for TMS web services.

  • Perform local operations and zonal summaries on raster data.

  • ... & more!
